Clam Chowder

Description

Lasagna is a hearty Italian dish known for its rich layers of flavor and satisfying textures. To prepare a classic lasagna, start by making a robust meat sauce. Sauté diced onions and garlic in olive oil until fragrant, then add ground beef or Italian sausage and cook until browned. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and a blend of Italian herbs like basil, oregano, and thyme. Simmer the sauce to deepen the flavors. For an added touch, incorporate a splash of red wine or a pinch of red pepper flakes for heat. While the sauce cooks, prepare the cheese filling by combining ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan, an egg, and freshly chopped parsley in a bowl.

To assemble, spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a baking dish, followed by a layer of uncooked lasagna noodles. Top the noodles with a portion of the ricotta mixture, then sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese over it. Repeat the layering process until all ingredients are used, finishing with a generous layer of mozzarella and Parmesan on top. Cover the dish with foil and bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 25 minutes, then remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes to achieve a bubbly, golden crust. Allow the lasagna to rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing to ensure neat layers. This timeless recipe is perfect for family dinners or special occasions, offering a comforting meal that's as delicious as it is satisfying.
